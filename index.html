Biography

I specialize in Robotics Engineering, focusing on the design, programming, testing, and enhancement of robotic platforms. My particular interest lies in drones, where I tackle challenges related to component weight, wireless communications, and flight autonomy.

In my Thesis, I did a project on autonomous drone navigation. I personally engineered a drone capable of autonomous flight in the rainforest, relying solely on onboard computing. Navigating through the dense and cluttered rainforest presented unique challenges, for example the rapid degradation of radio signals due to the thick vegetation. Consequently, the drone operated without the need of GPS and did not require a continuous connection with the Ground Station.

My passion for experimentation extends to building robots and control systems. You can see this in my internship and hobby projects. Utilizing embedded systems like Arduino and Raspberry Pi, I create innovative robotic solutions.

As a hobbyist drone pilot with a keen interest in aerial photography, I personally piloted my experimental drones during both indoor and outdoor test flights. I like piloting challenges, such as BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) and FPV (First Person View) flights. For more details of my piloting experiences, visit my Piloting page.

Across my diverse projects, I have sharpened my skills in component selection (sensors/actuators), embedded programming, field testing, and the system integration of robotic platforms.
