Andreas Zwanenburg

(MSc) Robotics Engineer

TU/Delft

I am a Robotics Engineer who likes to design, program, test, and improve robotic platforms. In particularly, I have in intrest in drones as these have many challenges such as component weight, wireless communications, flight stability and more. In my Thesis, I worked on drone autonomy where I created a drone myself that could fly autonomously in the rainforest with only onboard computing. Appart from the dense and cluttered environment for navigation, the dense vegetation also degrades radio signals rapidly. Therefore, the drone had to navigate without GPS, and was not relying on a permanent connection with the Ground Station.

Further I like experimenting with robots. During internship and hobby projects I regularly built robots or control systems based on embedded systems such as Arduino and Raspberry Pi.

I am a drone pilot for hobby photography and also piloted all experimental drones myself during testflights indoors and outdoors. I love piloting challenges such as BVLOS or FPV flights. See my Piloting page for my experience.

Over my projects alltogheter, I gained quite some skills in component choice (sensors/actuators), embedded programming, field testing, design and system integration of robotic platforms.
